FinanceAlytics (Ongoing Project)

Team Haofei Xu Kaitlyn Agnitti Mahir Thakkar Raja Sekhar Pietro Bartocci Sophia Ng

Tools Miro Figma Adobe Photoshop Stages Ideate Prototype Implement

The client company aims to create an investment educational analytic tool that gives users a sense of holistic endowment in investing as it mines, compiles, and normalizes countless hours of financial research into a decision-ready display, accompanied by educational elements. This product will be a web-based software platform or app. At the core of the product is a bundle of benefits, which are confidence in investing and the convenience of gathering data at one's fingertips. For now, none of the competitors has both educational components and the data analytics tool to compare company financial health side-by-side. Given such an opportunity, we are looking to develop a minimal viable product by building prototypes and testing with potential clients and stakeholders.

Stage 1 Understand In this part, We conducted multifaceted researches to better understand our problem space. First, we shared our prior knowledge about the context, the problem and listed a few existing apps and services. Next, we reflected on our own identities and how they can influence what we observe, interpret, and elicit from people and situations. We then created a list of our assumptions and biases on the context and refined our topic. Finally, we held five semi-structured interviews with participants recognized as the users or bystanders. Stage 2 Synthesize In this part, we first debriefed the notes from the interview, namely breaking them down into simply key data and putting them on sticky notes. As you can see from the board below, some participants are frequent bus users, some are active walkers and some others own a car bus still use buses. We intentionally recruited them to create a diverse sample so that we could understand the needs of different groups of users. Debrief Using sticky notes from the above debriefing board, we then organized them to find patterns. We found that users often switch between a few apps in order to get to their destination. For example, they used Google Maps to plan their trip and another real-time bus information app to track the bus. Another thing that drew our attention was those safety protocols during the COVID-19 pandemic. Students often need to show bus drivers their Building Access Status through another app. Moreover, first-year students often had difficulty getting to their classroom within the building and hoped some app could lead them to it. Seasonal factors also influenced users' experience. For example, snowy days made it hard to walk outside since it was slippery and cold. However, buses were more likely to delay and made users stressed, especially when they head to a meeting or class. Organize Using patterns found from the Organize board, we developed four insights...
